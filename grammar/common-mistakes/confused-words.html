üìö What are Confused Words?

Confused words are words that sound similar, look similar, or are commonly mixed up. Learning the differences will make your writing clearer and more accurate!

üéØ Commonly Confused Words

Their / There / They're

Their = possessive (belonging to them)

‚úì That's their house.

There = place or to introduce something

‚úì Put it over there.

‚úì There are five apples.

They're = they are (contraction)

‚úì They're going to the park.

Your / You're

Your = possessive (belonging to you)

‚úì Is this your backpack?

You're = you are (contraction)

‚úì You're my best friend.

Its / It's

Its = possessive (belonging to it)

‚úì The dog wagged its tail.

It's = it is or it has (contraction)

‚úì It's raining outside.

To / Too / Two

To = direction, part of infinitive verb

‚úì I'm going to school.

‚úì I want to play.

Too = also, or excessively

‚úì I want to come too!

‚úì It's too hot outside.

Two = the number 2

‚úì I have two cats.

Than / Then

Than = comparison

‚úì She is taller than me.

‚úì I like pizza more than pasta.

Then = time, sequence

‚úì We ate dinner, then watched a movie.

‚úì I was younger then.

Affect / Effect

Affect = verb (to influence)

‚úì The weather will affect our plans.

Effect = noun (result)

‚úì The medicine had a positive effect.

Accept / Except

Accept = verb (to receive, agree to)

‚úì I accept your apology.

Except = preposition (excluding)

‚úì Everyone came except Sarah.

Lose / Loose

Lose = verb (to misplace, not win)

‚úì Don't lose your keys!

‚úì Our team might lose the game.

Loose = adjective (not tight)

‚úì These pants are too loose.

Who's / Whose

Who's = who is or who has (contraction)

‚úì Who's coming to the party?

Whose = possessive (belonging to whom)

‚úì Whose book is this?

A Lot / Alot

A lot = TWO WORDS (many, much)

‚úì I have a lot of homework.

Alot = NOT A WORD!

‚ùå I have alot of homework.

By / Buy / Bye

By = preposition (near, before, through)

‚úì Stand by the door.

‚úì Finish by Friday.

Buy = verb (to purchase)

‚úì I want to buy a new phone.

Bye = goodbye

‚úì Bye! See you later!

Principal / Principle

Principal = head of school, or main/most important

‚úì The principal gave a speech.

‚úì The principal reason is cost.

Principle = rule, belief, fundamental truth

‚úì She has strong moral principles.

Weather / Whether

Weather = atmospheric conditions

‚úì The weather is nice today.

Whether = if (choice between alternatives)

‚úì I don't know whether to go or stay.

Lay / Lie

Lay = verb (to put something down) - needs an object

‚úì Please lay the book on the table.

‚úì She laid the blanket on the bed.

Lie = verb (to recline) - no object

‚úì I need to lie down.

‚úì The dog lay in the sun. (past tense)

Sit / Set

Sit = verb (to be seated) - no object

‚úì Please sit down.

Set = verb (to place something) - needs an object

‚úì Please set the plates on the table.

Advice / Advise

Advice = noun (suggestions, recommendations)

‚úì Thank you for your advice.

Advise = verb (to give advice)

‚úì I advise you to study hard.

üéØ Key Takeaways

  • Contractions use apostrophes: it's, you're, they're, who's
  • Possessives (without apostrophes for pronouns): its, your, their, whose
  • Than = comparison, then = time
  • Too = also/excessively, to = direction/infinitive, two = number
  • Affect = verb (influence), effect = noun (result)
  • Accept = receive, except = excluding
  • A lot is TWO WORDS (alot is not a word!)
  • When in doubt, break contractions apart to check meaning
