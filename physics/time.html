⏱️ What is Time?

Time measures how long something takes to happen. In the study of motion, time tells us how long an object has been moving. Together with speed and distance, time helps us describe exactly how things travel from one place to another!

📐

Measuring Time

We measure time using clocks, stopwatches, and timers. The units we use depend on how long something lasts:

📝 Units of Time:

  • Seconds (s) — the basic unit scientists use for motion
  • Minutes (min) — 1 minute = 60 seconds
  • Hours (h) — 1 hour = 60 minutes = 3,600 seconds
🧮

Finding Time from Distance and Speed

If you know the distance and the speed, you can work out how long the journey takes:

🧮 Time Formula:

\( \text{Time} = \dfrac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}} \)

\( t = \dfrac{d}{s} \)

📝 Let's Try It:

If you need to travel 100 meters and you run at 5 m/s, the time it takes is:

\( \dfrac{100\ \text{m}}{5\ \text{m/s}} = 20\ \text{seconds} \)

It would take you 20 seconds to reach the finish line!

🔺

The Motion Triangle

Speed, distance, and time are like three best friends. If you know any two of them, you can always find the third!

💡 One Family of Formulas:

These three formulas all come from the same idea:

  • \( \text{Speed} = \dfrac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}} \)
  • \( \text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time} \)
  • \( \text{Time} = \dfrac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}} \)
⚡ Fun Fact: The most accurate clocks in the world are atomic clocks. They are so precise they would only lose one second every 100 million years!
