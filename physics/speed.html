🏃 What is Speed?

Speed tells us how fast something is moving. It measures how much distance an object covers in a certain amount of time. A cheetah running across the grass has a high speed, while a snail crawling along has a very low speed!

🧮

The Speed Formula

To find speed, we divide the distance traveled by the time it took:

🧮 Speed Formula:

Speed = Distance ÷ Time

s = d ÷ t

Speed is usually measured in meters per second (m/s) or kilometers per hour (km/h).

📝 Let's Try It:

If you ride your bike 100 meters in 20 seconds, your speed is:

100 m ÷ 20 s = 5 m/s

That means you travel 5 meters every single second!

💡

Think of it Like This

💡 The Race Idea:

Imagine two friends racing to the same tree. The one who gets there in less time was moving faster — they had a higher speed. Speed is all about covering distance quickly!

📝 Real-World Speeds:

  • A person walking: about 1.4 m/s (5 km/h)
  • A person running fast: about 8 m/s (29 km/h)
  • A car on the highway: about 28 m/s (100 km/h)
  • A jet airplane: about 250 m/s (900 km/h)
  • Light: a super-speedy 300,000,000 m/s!
⚡ Fun Fact: The fastest land animal, the cheetah, can reach a speed of about 30 m/s (over 100 km/h) — but only for short bursts!
🧭

Speed vs. Velocity

Sometimes scientists use the word velocity. Velocity is just like speed, but it also tells you the direction you're moving!

  • Speed: "I'm going 5 m/s." (just how fast)
  • Velocity: "I'm going 5 m/s toward the north." (how fast AND which way)

📚 Quick Summary

🏃 Speed

How fast something moves — distance divided by time

🧮 Formula

Speed = Distance ÷ Time (s = d ÷ t)

📐 Units

Measured in m/s or km/h

🧭 Velocity

Speed in a particular direction
