SOH · CAH · TOA

In a right triangle, the three main trig ratios connect an angle to the sides. Remember them with SOH-CAH-TOA:

The names of the sides depend on angle θ.

SOH
\(\sin\theta = \dfrac{\text{Opp}}{\text{Hyp}}\)
CAH
\(\cos\theta = \dfrac{\text{Adj}}{\text{Hyp}}\)
TOA
\(\tan\theta = \dfrac{\text{Opp}}{\text{Adj}}\)

Hypotenuse

The longest side

Always opposite the right angle (90°).

Opposite

Across from angle θ

The side that does not touch the angle you are using.

Adjacent

Next to angle θ

The side touching θ that is not the hypotenuse.

Pythagorean Theorem

\(a^2 + b^2 = c^2\)

c is the hypotenuse. Find a missing side without angles.

Finding a Missing Angle

Use the inverse functions on your calculator:

Inverse Sine

\(\theta = \sin^{-1}\!\left(\dfrac{\text{opp}}{\text{hyp}}\right)\)

Inverse Cosine

\(\theta = \cos^{-1}\!\left(\dfrac{\text{adj}}{\text{hyp}}\right)\)

Inverse Tangent

\(\theta = \tan^{-1}\!\left(\dfrac{\text{opp}}{\text{adj}}\right)\)

Worked Example

A right triangle has an angle of 30° and a hypotenuse of 10. Find the side opposite the 30° angle.

1. We know the angle and the hypotenuse, and want the opposite → use SOH.
2. \(\sin 30^\circ = \dfrac{\text{opposite}}{10}\)
3. \(\text{opposite} = 10 \times \sin 30^\circ = 10 \times 0.5\)
4. \(\text{opposite} = 5\)

Reciprocal & Quotient Identities

Reciprocals

\(\csc\theta = \dfrac{1}{\sin\theta}\) \(\sec\theta = \dfrac{1}{\cos\theta}\) \(\cot\theta = \dfrac{1}{\tan\theta}\)

Quotients

\(\tan\theta = \dfrac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}\) \(\cot\theta = \dfrac{\cos\theta}{\sin\theta}\)

Pythagorean Identities

Main Identity

\(\sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1\)

Here \(\sin^2\theta\) means \((\sin\theta)^2\).

Tangent Form

\(1 + \tan^2\theta = \sec^2\theta\)

Cotangent Form

\(1 + \cot^2\theta = \csc^2\theta\)

Double-Angle Formulas

Sine

\(\sin 2\theta = 2\sin\theta\cos\theta\)

Cosine

\(\cos 2\theta = \cos^2\theta - \sin^2\theta\) \(\cos 2\theta = 2\cos^2\theta - 1\) \(\cos 2\theta = 1 - 2\sin^2\theta\)

Tangent

\(\tan 2\theta = \dfrac{2\tan\theta}{1 - \tan^2\theta}\)

Half-Angle Formulas

Sine

\(\sin\dfrac{\theta}{2} = \pm\sqrt{\dfrac{1 - \cos\theta}{2}}\)

Cosine

\(\cos\dfrac{\theta}{2} = \pm\sqrt{\dfrac{1 + \cos\theta}{2}}\)

Tangent

\(\tan\dfrac{\theta}{2} = \dfrac{1 - \cos\theta}{\sin\theta}\)

Angle Sum & Difference

Sine

\(\sin(A \pm B) = \sin A\cos B \pm \cos A\sin B\)

Cosine

\(\cos(A \pm B) = \cos A\cos B \mp \sin A\sin B\)

Tangent

\(\tan(A \pm B) = \dfrac{\tan A \pm \tan B}{1 \mp \tan A\tan B}\)

Laws for Any Triangle

These work for all triangles, not just right triangles. Sides \(a, b, c\) are opposite angles \(A, B, C\).

Law of Sines

\(\dfrac{a}{\sin A} = \dfrac{b}{\sin B} = \dfrac{c}{\sin C}\)

Law of Cosines

\(c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab\cos C\)

Symmetry & Co-Function Rules

Even / Odd

\(\cos(-\theta) = \cos\theta\) \(\sin(-\theta) = -\sin\theta\) \(\tan(-\theta) = -\tan\theta\)

Co-Functions

\(\sin(90^\circ - \theta) = \cos\theta\) \(\cos(90^\circ - \theta) = \sin\theta\)

Worked Example: Double Angle

If \(\sin\theta = 0.6\) and \(\cos\theta = 0.8\), find \(\sin 2\theta\).

1. Use the formula: \(\sin 2\theta = 2\sin\theta\cos\theta\)
2. Substitute: \(\sin 2\theta = 2 \times 0.6 \times 0.8\)
3. Answer: \(\sin 2\theta = 0.96\)
