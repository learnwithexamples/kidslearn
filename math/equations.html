The Balance Rule

An equation is like a balance scale: the left side always equals the right side. Whatever you do to one side, you must do to the other side too — then it stays balanced.

Inverse Operations

+ undoes − × undoes ÷

To move a number, do the opposite operation to both sides.

One-Step

\(x + 3 = 7\) \(x = 7 - 3 = 4\)

Subtract 3 from both sides.

Two-Step

\(2x + 3 = 11\) \(2x = 8 \;\Rightarrow\; x = 4\)

Undo the \(+3\) first, then the \(\times 2\).

Variables on Both Sides

\(3x + 2 = x + 10\) \(2x = 8 \;\Rightarrow\; x = 4\)

Collect the \(x\) terms on one side first.

Worked Example

Solve \(3x + 2 = x + 10\)

1. Start: \(3x + 2 = x + 10\)
2. Subtract \(x\) from both sides: \(2x + 2 = 10\)
3. Subtract \(2\) from both sides: \(2x = 8\)
4. Divide both sides by \(2\): \(x = 4\)
5. Check: \(3(4) + 2 = 14\) and \(4 + 10 = 14\) ✓

Tips

  • Do the same thing to both sides every time.
  • Undo addition/subtraction first, then multiplication/division.
  • Collect all the \(x\) terms on one side and the numbers on the other.
  • Always check by putting your answer back into the original equation.

Keep it balanced! ⚖️
